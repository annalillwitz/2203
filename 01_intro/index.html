### Week 1: Introduction ## Monday: We got into groups and began discussing different aspects of design that could be used for our solar cooker prototype. I worked with Brittany McHenry on discussing how we could create mirrors. We researched how we could grind a small mirror out of glass to use as a satellite mirror. We also discussed how to build a turntable. This could be used to pour epoxy in a parabolic shape, which could then be covered by a reflective material. We could use this as our main parabolic mirror. We also worked on creating a website to use in this class. I created a github repository but was not able to get it working. Here is a [link to the website with instructions on how to grind a mirror by hand](https://www.instructables.com/Grind-and-Polish-a-DobsonianNewtonian-Telescope-Mi/). ## Wednesday: We looked at various examples of solar cookers bought from Amazon.com. We got into groups to help assemble two of them. I worked on the yellow folding solar cooker. After this one was fully built, we took the yellow folding solar cooker and another one (which I will refer to as the pre-assembled one) outside to test them. We put an equal amount of water in both of them and recorded the temperatures of the water at various times. It was determined that the pre-assembled solar cooker was more effective than the yellow folding one at heating up water. However, we hypothesized that using a black pot instead of a metallic one to hold the water for the yellow solar cooker might change the results a little.
